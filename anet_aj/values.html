03 · Brand values

Five core
brand values.

Before designing anything, I defined the values together with the owners. These five pillars then guided every visual decision — from the logo curve to the wine-red on the loyalty card.

01
Honesty

Real ingredients from small Italian producers. No marketing claims, no shortcuts. The brand should look as honest as the food tastes.

02
Human touch

The brand has faces — Nebo and Karolína. It is not an anonymous business. Communication uses a personal voice, not corporate language.

03
La Dolce Vita

A relaxed, joyful way of eating. People laugh at the table. Food brings them together. The aesthetic refers to Italian retro design from the 1960s.

04
Local identity

The brand belongs to Roztoky. The winding road of the town is part of the visual language — it appears in the logo and in the illustration style.

05
Visual nostalgia

The aesthetic is rooted in Italian retro design — playful, warm, and welcoming. Halftone textures, grain, and Thunder typography support this feeling.

How the values drive the design

The values are not decoration — they are the brief. Every visual element traces back to one of them:

HONESTY →
Material choices

Cream and off-white papers. Recyclable packaging. Photography that shows ingredients, not retouched studio shots.

HUMAN →
Tone of voice

Short sentences. First-person plural ("we"). Italian phrases used naturally, not forced. Texts written for real people, not for SEO.

DOLCE VITA →
Typography

Thunder font for headlines — directly inspired by 1960s Italian film posters. Space Mono for details — like a typed receipt.

LOCAL →
The logo curve

The wavy tail of the pizza slice mirrors the road of Roztoky. The brand is tied to a real geographical place, not a stock idea of Italy.

NOSTALGIA →
Visual treatment

Halftone filters on Instagram photos. Grain texture across the system. Hand-drawn icons instead of vector illustrations.

ALL FIVE →
Consistency

Same olive green on the façade, the box, the menu, the website. Same icons across print and digital. A guest sees one coherent brand.

A brand, not a logo

A logo on its own is not a brand. A brand is the sum of every decision a customer can see, hear, or feel. The values define what those decisions should add up to.

If a guest walks past the green façade, smells the oven through the door, picks up a menu with Space Mono pricing, hears the staff use "we" and "ciao" naturally, and then takes home a box that looks like a small gift — they experience the brand even before they think about it. That is the goal of identity work.
